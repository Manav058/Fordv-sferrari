About Honesty

In the 1960s, the Ford Motor Company is struggling to keep up with the innovative and dominating presence of Ferrari in the world of motorsport. Seeking to revitalize the company's image and assert American engineering prowess, Henry Ford II decides to challenge Ferrari at their own game by entering the prestigious 24 Hours of Le Mans race.To achieve this ambitious goal, Ford enlists the help of automotive visionary Carroll Shelby, a former race car driver turned designer and entrepreneur. Shelby is tasked with building a revolutionary racing car capable of defeating Ferrari's formidable team at Le Mans. He recruits the fiercely talented but volatile British driver Ken Miles to pilot the car, despite resistance from Ford's executives who doubt Miles' suitability for the job.





Shelby and Miles embark on a grueling journey to develop and test the new racing car, the Ford GT40. They encounter numerous challenges along the way, including technical setbacks, corporate interference, and personal conflicts. Despite these obstacles, Shelby and Miles form a deep bond built on mutual respect and a shared passion for racing.




As the pressure mounts, Shelby and Miles face fierce competition from Ferrari and their legendary driver, Enzo Ferrari. The rivalry between the two automotive giants intensifies, fueled by egos, corporate agendas, and the desire for glory.





On race day at Le Mans, Shelby, Miles, and the Ford team must overcome mechanical failures, strategic setbacks, and the relentless pursuit of victory from their Italian rivals. In a heart-stopping climax, Miles pushes the GT40 to its limits, battling against the clock and the elements to secure Ford's place in racing history.





In the end, "Ford v Ferrari" is not just a story of speed and competition but a testament to the power of innovation, teamwork, and the indomitable human spirit to overcome the odds and achieve greatness on and off the racetrack.
